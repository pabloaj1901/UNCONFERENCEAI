08 d2 Dynamic ② 9:15 AM · 20 min · Table activation

AI Types · 9:52 AM
14 s2 AI Type 1/5 9:52 AM · ~3 min
Predictive AI — patrones hacia el futuro
El tipo más silencioso y más valioso para el PMO. Muchos ya lo usan sin llamarlo AI.
Qué decir

"Type one: Predictive AI."

"This is the foundation. It learns patterns from historical data to estimate what's likely to happen next."

"It doesn't follow rules the way automation does. It finds signals in past data before they show up on anyone's radar."

"For a PMO: imagine a system that flags a project as at-risk before any PM raises their hand. That's predictive AI."

"The watch-out is on screen: garbage in, garbage out. Weak or limited historical data produces weak predictions. This one only works if your data is clean enough."

"When does it fit? When you have historical project data, patterns that repeat across projects, and volume too high to monitor manually."

15 s3 AI Type 2/5 9:55 AM · ~3 min
Generative AI — contenido desde instrucciones
Lo que la mayoría tiene en mente cuando dice "AI" hoy. Importante separar fluidez de precisión.
Qué decir

"Type two: Generative AI."

"This is what most people mean today when they say AI. It creates content — text, summaries, drafts, emails, plans — from natural language instructions."

"For a PMO: drafting status reports, turning meeting notes into executive summaries, synthesizing updates from multiple projects."

"The watch-out is critical: fluent output can still be wrong. These models sound confident even when they're not accurate. Always review before sending."

"And this one I'll say clearly: generating content is not the same as creating value. The question is always — does the output move something that matters?"

"When does it fit? When unstructured input needs to become structured output, and the first draft is the real bottleneck."

16 s4 AI Type 3/5 9:58 AM · ~2 min
Conversational AI — diálogo natural
Más cercano al día a día de los equipos. El riesgo menos obvio: la fluidez del diálogo esconde profundidad superficial.
Qué decir

"Type three: Conversational AI."

"Designed to respond through chat or voice in a back-and-forth exchange. It feels like a conversation because it is one."

"For a PMO: a project assistant that answers questions, gives status updates, and guides people — in plain language, 24/7."

"The watch-out: good dialogue can hide shallow understanding. The answer sounds right, sounds fluent — but verify anything critical before acting on it."

"When does it fit? When information exists but is hard to navigate, and repetitive questions are consuming team time."

17 s5 AI Type 4/5 10:00 AM · ~2 min
Agentic AI — acción autónoma multi-paso
El más poderoso y el más exigente en términos de governance. Introduce la idea de control como prerequisito.
Qué decir

"Type four: Agentic AI."

"This one doesn't wait to be asked. It plans, acts, and coordinates across systems — autonomously."

"For a PMO: gathering updates from multiple sources, consolidating them, flagging risks, routing actions — without a human orchestrating each step."

"The watch-out is the most important one in this list: weak controls create errors nobody intended. The more autonomous the system, the more important the guardrails."

"This is not a tool you deploy and forget. It requires clear ownership, explicit boundaries, and someone with authority to say 'this is mine and I'm watching it.'"

"When does it fit? When multi-step workflows run on schedule, coordination spans multiple systems, and human orchestration has become the bottleneck."

18 s6 AI Type 5/5 10:02 AM · ~2 min
RPA — automatización de reglas fijas
La herramienta más madura y más frágil. Importante ser honesto: RPA no aprende.
Qué decir

"Type five: RPA — Robotic Process Automation."

"This one is different from the others. RPA does not learn. It follows fixed rules across systems — the same steps, every time."

"For a PMO: moving data between systems, assembling standard reports, same structure every cycle."

"The watch-out says everything: process changes break the automation immediately. It knows one path. Change a step — it fails."

"So RPA is powerful and brittle at the same time. It earns its place when the rules are fixed, the data is structured, and the process is stable and well-documented."

"Many PMOs already use this — sometimes without calling it AI."

24 f2 Framework ⑦ 11:10 AM · 15 min · Selection Criteria + Two-Track
Selection Criteria + Two-Track logic
El framework más importante de la sesión. Introduces los 5 criterios y el Two-Track. Dale peso — aquí está la lógica que van a usar en el ejercicio final.
Criterios de selección

"To help you make that choice — five criteria. Use them to evaluate your top opportunity."

"Pain intensity. Does it solve a problem that actually hurts right now?"

"Data readiness. Is the data clean, available, and owned by someone?"

"Adoption feasibility. Can your team actually use this — not in theory, in practice?"

"Strategic alignment. Does it connect to something leadership already cares about?"

"Leadership credibility. Can you explain it in executive language and have it land?"

pausa

"If something scores low on two or more of these — it may still be a good idea. But it's not a near-term bet."

"The goal is not to kill ideas. It's to stop confusing interesting ideas with defendable ones."

Two-Track — la lógica paralela

"Now — one more thing before the exercise. And this one matters."

"There's a trap most organizations fall into with AI."

"You either chase the quick visible win — something that gets attention, something impressive…"

"…or you go straight for the long-term foundation — the slow work, the structural work, the work nobody applauds."

"And most organizations choose one."

"The Two-Track says: don't choose. Run both. In parallel."

"Track 1 is the Quick Win. Something visible in 30 to 60 days. Not an endless pilot. A real, small result that someone with authority can look at and say: this works, let's keep going."

"Its job is not transformation. Its job is credibility."

"Track 2 is the Strategic Foundation. The slow work. Data cleanup. Process clarity. Explicit ownership. Someone who says: this is mine, I'm driving it."

"And here's the critical point:"

"If your Quick Win works but the Foundation is not running in parallel — you don't have a scalable solution. You have an orphaned demo."

"It works once. It impresses. And then it dies."

"Same opportunity. Two tracks. Run in parallel. That's the difference between experimentation and transformation."

25 f3 Dynamic ⑧ 11:25 AM · 35 min · Executive Pressure
Executive Pressure — defiende tu apuesta
El ejercicio más exigente. Metes presión ejecutiva real. Tu tono cambia aquí — eres más duro, más directo.
Setup del ejercicio

"Now imagine this."

"Your CFO, your CEO, or your PMO sponsor looks at you and says:"

cambias tono — más directo, más seco

"'Fine. You have budget for one AI move this year. Why this one?'"

pausa

"That's the question. And now your table has to answer it."

Instrucciones

"Three things to do at your table."

"First: rate your top three opportunities against the five criteria — high, medium, low. Two or more lows? Off the near-term agenda."

"Second: apply the Two-Track. For your strongest idea — what's the Quick Win? What's the Strategic Foundation? Who owns each one?"

"Third, last 5 minutes: name the biggest risk to your two-track approach. Not the risk you like talking about. The real one."

"You have 30 minutes. Make it count."

Mientras caminas
  • Busca las mesas que están siendo demasiado amables con sus propias ideas. Empuja: "What's the honest score on data readiness — not the optimistic one?"
  • Si una mesa tiene dos lows en los criterios: "That's a signal. Is this a near-term bet or a future aspiration?"
  • La pregunta de riesgo es la más importante. Si alguien da un riesgo vago: "Make it concrete. What specifically breaks first?"
